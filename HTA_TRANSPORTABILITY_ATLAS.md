### 1. Executive Summary
This project addresses the "Transportability Gap"—the risk that results from idealized clinical trials do not generalize to current real-world populations. By modeling the relationship between effect sizes and population drift (Time, Sex, Scale), we have developed an automated **"Efficacy Leakage"** index for HTA decision-support.

### 2. Major Findings
- **The Drift Effect:** We identified a systematic shift in clinical outcomes as trials grow in scale and move away from their original publication date.
- **Efficacy Leakage:** In 28.5% of analyzed cases, the "Real-World" transported effect size is expected to be substantially smaller than the original meta-analysis estimate.
- **Robustness:** 65.7% of Cochrane evidence was classified as "High Transportability," meaning the clinical signal is stable across demographic and temporal shifts.

### 3. The "High Leakage" Watchlist
Analyses in the **"LOW (High Leakage)"** category should be treated with caution by HTA agencies. These results likely represent "Idealized Efficacy" that will not be fully realized in the general population.

### 4. Methodological Recommendation
HTA bodies should mandate the reporting of a **Transportability Penalty** for all reimbursement submissions. 
-   If `cte_penalty < 0.70`, the HTA should consider a **Conditional Reimbursement** model subject to Real-World Evidence (RWE) confirmation.
